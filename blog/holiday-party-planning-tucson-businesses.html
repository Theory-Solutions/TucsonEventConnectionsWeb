How to Plan an Exceptional Holiday Party for Your Tucson Business

The company holiday party is more than a seasonal tradition — it is one of the most important culture-building events a business can host. Done well, it boosts morale, strengthens team bonds, and sends employees into the new year feeling appreciated and energized. Done poorly, it is something people tolerate rather than enjoy.

Tucson businesses have access to a wonderful range of venues and vendors to create a holiday party your team will actually look forward to. Here is a complete planning guide to make it happen.

Set Your Budget Early

Holiday party budgets vary widely. Common per-person benchmarks in Tucson:

  • Budget celebration: $25-$50 per person (food and drinks at a casual venue)
  • Mid-range event: $75-$150 per person (full catering at a dedicated event space)
  • Premium gala: $150-$300+ per person (resort venue, full service, entertainment)

Determine your total headcount early. A 50-person company at $100 per head is a $5,000 event. Knowing your number before you start shopping prevents sticker shock.

Choose the Right Format

The best holiday party format depends on your company culture:

  • Catered dinner or cocktail party — Classic, works for most companies
  • Activity-based event — Bowling, cooking class, escape room, trivia night
  • Lunch celebration — Daytime option for companies where evening is difficult
  • Outdoor barbecue or picnic — Casual, Tucson's mild winters make this viable
  • Volunteer event + celebration — Meaningful and morale-boosting

Venue Selection for Tucson Business Holiday Parties

Tucson has excellent holiday party venue options:

  • Hotel event spaces — Reliable, full service, scalable from 20 to 500 people
  • Restaurant buyouts — Great for smaller teams (10-50), food-focused experience
  • Private event halls — Maximum flexibility, great for themed events
  • Outdoor resort spaces — Tucson's mild December weather makes outdoor events viable at many of the city's resort properties
  • Rooftop venues — Several Tucson venues offer rooftop spaces with city views
"The holiday party should feel like a gift, not an obligation. The difference is in the details — good food, genuine warmth, and experiences that feel intentional rather than generic."

Catering for Holiday Parties

Popular Tucson holiday catering options:

  • Traditional holiday fare (roasted meats, seasonal sides, holiday desserts)
  • Sonoran Mexican buffet — always a crowd favorite in Tucson
  • Passed appetizers and cocktail stations for standing receptions
  • Interactive food stations (carving station, dessert bar, taco bar)

Entertainment and Activities

  • Live music or DJ
  • Photo booth with holiday-themed props
  • White elephant or gift exchange
  • Trivia night with company-themed questions
  • Award ceremony recognizing employee achievements
  • Charity raffle benefiting a local Tucson organization

Inclusivity Considerations

Modern holiday parties should be welcoming to all team members. Consider:

  • Non-alcoholic beverage options that are equally festive
  • Dietary accommodations (vegan, gluten-free, halal)
  • Accessible venue for employees with mobility needs
  • Inclusive framing ("holiday party" rather than a specific religious celebration)

Holiday Party Essentials

Make the space feel festive with quality decorations. A corporate holiday party decoration set and quality warm indoor string lights can transform any event space into a winter celebration without requiring a professional decorator.

Timeline for Planning

  • September: Set budget, confirm date, begin venue search
  • October: Book venue and caterer
  • November: Book entertainment, send invitations, confirm dietary needs
  • First week of December: Confirm headcount, finalize menu
  • Week before: Confirm all vendors, finalize run-of-show
