Why You Need a Checklist for Your Tucson Event

Planning an event in Tucson means juggling vendors, venues, weather, guest logistics, and a hundred small details — all at once. A solid checklist keeps you on track and makes sure nothing slips through the cracks, whether you're planning a wedding for 200 or a quinceañera for 80.

12+ Months Before the Event

  • Set your total budget and allocate by category
  • Decide on a general date range (avoid Tucson's peak summer heat: June–August)
  • Create your initial guest list to determine venue size
  • Start researching and touring Tucson venues
  • Book your venue as soon as you decide — popular venues fill up fast
  • Hire a wedding/event planner if using one

9–12 Months Before

  • Book your photographer and videographer (they book out earliest)
  • Book your DJ or live band
  • Begin catering consultations and tastings
  • Start dress/attire shopping (alterations take time)
  • Send save-the-dates
  • Research and book florist

6–9 Months Before

  • Finalize catering menu and headcount estimate
  • Book hair and makeup artists
  • Order invitations and stationery
  • Plan transportation (shuttle, limo, etc.)
  • Book hotel room blocks for out-of-town guests
  • Confirm all vendor contracts are signed

3–6 Months Before

  • Send formal invitations (6–8 weeks before event)
  • Finalize ceremony details and programs
  • Schedule final fittings
  • Create a detailed day-of timeline
  • Plan rehearsal dinner (if applicable)
  • Order wedding/event cake or desserts

1–3 Months Before

  • Confirm RSVPs and give final headcount to caterer
  • Confirm all vendor arrival times and logistics
  • Create seating chart
  • Prepare final payments for vendors (many require payment 2–4 weeks prior)
  • Break in your shoes!
  • Delegate day-of tasks to trusted people

1–2 Weeks Before

  • Final walkthrough with venue coordinator
  • Confirm final guest count with all vendors
  • Prepare day-of emergency kit (safety pins, stain remover, pain reliever, etc.)
  • Charge all electronics (phone, battery packs)
  • Confirm transportation logistics

Day Before

  • Rehearsal and rehearsal dinner
  • Deliver items to venue (favors, decorations, signage)
  • Prepare all vendor payments (envelopes with cash or checks)
  • Get a good night's sleep!

Day Of

  • Have point-of-contact handle vendor questions (not you)
  • Keep a copy of all vendor contacts on hand
  • Eat breakfast — seriously
  • Start getting ready earlier than you think you need to
  • Enjoy every moment

Recommended Planning Tools (Amazon Picks)

  • Wedding/Event Planner Binders — keep all your contracts, notes, and timelines organized
  • Day-Of Emergency Kits — pre-packed kits with everything you might need
  • Portable Phone Chargers — your phone will die on event day without one
